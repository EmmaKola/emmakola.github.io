# Recommendations

- **Hydrochlorothiazide, Montelukast, and Amoxicillin** should undergo annual pricing evaluations to ensure optimal margin performance, particularly given their high-volume but lower-revenue profile.

- **Lisinopril**, as a top-performing product, requires a structured price-review cycle, supported by supplier benchmarking to secure lower procurement costs and protect long-term profitability.

- **Aspirin’s** exceptionally high profit margin positions it as a key efficiency driver. To maximise value, the business should either scale sales volume through targeted demand-generation initiatives or enhance profitability via pricing optimisation and cost-efficiency measures.

- **Male customers** contribute a disproportionately large share of total revenue, indicating a high-value demographic segment. Strengthening engagement through tailored marketing strategies and product positioning will help sustain and expand this revenue stream.

- Revenue opportunities within the **‘Other’ gender** segment should be explored, as this group may represent an under-penetrated market with growth potential.

- With over **88% of revenue** generated from reseller channels, Nova Meds should prioritise retention strategies—such as competitive pricing, loyalty incentives, and value-added partnerships—to safeguard market share and reduce competitive risk.
